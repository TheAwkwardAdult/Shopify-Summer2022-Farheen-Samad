One-Stop Shop for conscious buying- GoodBuy

Introduction

Fast fashion is a popular and exploitative business model based on replicating high-fashion designs, mass-producing them at low cost. Oftentimes, this entails exploiting workers in inhumane conditions. Apparel workers are among the lowest-paid people in the world. Patagonia believes that every worker has the right to fair compensation and a living wage. As a founding member of the Fair Labor Association (FLA), Patagonia has embedded its living wage standard in its code of conduct and developed an approach to improving wages in its partner factories.

Adobe Creative Jam + Patagonia: The Design Challenge

The Solution - GoodBuy

GoodBuy facilitates conscious buying by helping users identify ethical brands that make a difference through their transparent fair trade factories and inside stories. A consumer can become an advocate of a fair trade when given them the opportunity to become more aware of the factory workers’ lives and communities.

The Process

Discovery

I did some secondary research to find out more about the current issues with transparency in brands and found the following listed insights interesting.

  • 90% of fashion brands don't disclose living wages.
  • Only 0.6% of the cost of a T Shirt goes to workers as pay.
  • There is very little or no involvement of consumers directly on the problems of factory workers.
  • There are several tools like Fashion Transparency Index which attempts at incentivizing large fashion brands to be more transparent about their social and environmental effort.
  • There are several Third Party Fair Trade regulating organizations to act against brands and supply chain practices that are not beneficial for factory workers.

Interviewing users

Now that I had a base information about the issues from brand side, I wanted to dive deeper into what goes on in the mind of consumer while they purchase clothes. I wanted to know their take on sustainability and factory worker's well being.
I interviewed 6 people in age range 23- 29.
I synthesized the data from secondary research and primary research to get insights by affinity mapping.

Research Insights

Awareness of action
  • Sustainable choices come second to price of clothes.
  • People don’t know what factors make brand sustainable.
  • Living wage of factory workers doesn’t directly connect to people.
  • People want a better way of knowing the positive impact of thier ethical choice.
Making brands accountable
  • Transparancy of supply chain is not a priority for brands.
  • Brands are not held accountable for thier unmindful actions.
  • Factory policies are not directly controlled by brands.
  • Well being of labours is secondary to profit for big brands.
Making factory worker's cause more visible
  • People can be instrumental in bringing changes in policies.
  • People can help brand care more about how they treat and pay workers.
  • People are willing to directly impact factory worker’s lives
unifying User's power
  • People’s impact are not unified enough to make considerable change in the system.
  • Impact of users on system can motivate the further to be conscious about thier choices.
  • More spotlight is given to the negative impacts of users instead of positive impacts

Key oppurtunity areas

01
Educate Users

Educate users about suitability and factors that makes brands ethical. Make users more aware about the supply chain policies of brands.

02
Encourage Users

focusing on positive impact of users and using it as a motivation for users to opt for ethical and sustainable options.

03
Empower users

Unifing user's contribution to make their impact powerful.

How it connects to my design philosophy?

Educating users helps users be more conscious and mindful of their actions. Encouraging users through focusing on the positive impacts of their actions and empowering users to give power to users and not to large clothing brands.

Ideation and Solution Process

Persona

Based on research and insight, I created a persona, to help us ideate solution.

Wireframing

Final UI Solution

Topical Stories about brands

Selected brands telling topical stories through their motto, their most valuable practices for workers and their most valuable products for consumers metrics

Behind the making of clothes

Bullet points on the clothes in making helps to attach emotion and gives clothes a depth.

About the Weaver

A brief note on the worker who made the clothes -a way to give recognition to the hard work that these factory workers put in, something that they don't get enough of; this also makes consumers aware of their lives.

Directly impact a worker's life

Option to donate a small amount of money that goes directly into the worker’s pocket.

Supporting Workers' cause

We also give the consumers the option to donate to certain campaigns run by the brands for the factory workers' causes.

Ethical Choice Edit

A monthly digital magazine that publishes inspiring stories about how GoodBuy’s and the brands’ contribution is making a positive impact on factory workers and their communities. 

Ethical Quotient and brand voting in Real-time.

The ethical quotient of the product in real-time and consumer voting for brands. These votes give consumers a certain amount of power when it comes to big commercial brands. We have brand recommendations based on metrics and user votes each week to ensure that the brands are more conscious about their impact on the environment and the treatment of their workers.

Mobilizing Customer support.

Option for non-monetary support through signing petitions that help third-party fair trade regulating organizations gain enough momentum to act against brands and supply chain practices that are not beneficial for factory workers.

Database of factories labeled as Fair Trade.

To know more about the measures these factories are taking for the well-being of their workers.

Becoming an advocate for factory workers

We also give the opportunity to the consumers to become more involved with the worker communities and how they can help the communities by donating.

Spotlight brands who care.

We also keep a track of brands that stand for the workers and their causes and make users aware of them.

Conscious User Journey Profile.

A profile for the users which gives a summary of their conscious journey through metrics like donation, positive environmental impact through their decision to buy ethical clothing, and also how their voting helped GoodBuy create top ethical brand lists.

  • "I loved the onboarding."

Future Scope

Reflections

  • Users are unaware of how their daily choices can have such huge impact on the environment and worker's lives.
  • Users are willing to opt for sustainable options if they know that their spending is making a positive impact.
  • There is a huge experience gap in our solution and the reality, as a designer, we need to work towards decreasing the gap.
